## What we're seeing

Across County Kerry, bus stops are listed in the National Transport Authority's journey planner and assigned official stop IDs — yet many of these stops have no pole, no flag, no shelter, and no sign at street level. A passenger using the journey planner to plan a trip has no reliable way to locate the stop when they arrive.

This is not a marginal issue. Rural stops are frequently unmarked. In some cases, the only way to know where to board is through local knowledge — a driveway, a gable wall, or a particular bend in the road — passed informally between neighbours.

## Why it matters

A bus network is only accessible if its stops can be found. Basic wayfinding — a pole and a flag with the stop ID — costs a small fraction of the annual operating cost of any route, yet its absence can make services feel unreliable and unwelcoming. The practical effect is that some people simply do not use buses they cannot locate.

From a planning perspective, unmarked stops are also hard to count and audit. Without a visible, consistent physical presence, it is difficult for communities or representatives to know which stops exist, which are active, and which have been removed or relocated.

## Possible improvements

Small changes can make a significant difference here:

- Fitting standard NTA bus stop poles and flags at all active stops
- Adding stop IDs to existing poles where flags are missing
- Updating the NTA stop register to reflect current physical conditions
- Including stop-location maps or coordinates in timetables and journey planners

None of these require new infrastructure — they require a consistent programme of checking and fitting.

## For representatives

This is an area where a straightforward question to the relevant transport authority can be productive. A list of stop IDs with no corresponding physical infrastructure is something that should be possible to obtain and to act on.

**Suggested council question:** *Can the council confirm how many bus stop locations within the county are registered in the NTA stop database but do not currently have a physical pole, flag, or sign, and what the timeline is for addressing the shortfall?*
