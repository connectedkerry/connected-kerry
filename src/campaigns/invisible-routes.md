## What we're seeing

Across Kerry, people walk and cycle along roads, lanes, and informal paths that official systems do not recognise as active travel routes. These routes are used daily — for school runs, to reach bus stops, to access town centres — yet they receive no maintenance priority, no safety improvement, and no usage counting.

Without recognition, there is no record that these routes exist, no case for improvement, and no baseline against which progress can be measured.

## Examples

- Rural roads used for walking to bus stops that have no footpath or road markings
- School routes along national or regional roads with no safe walking provision
- Informal paths and desire lines through farmland or open space that connect communities

<div class="callout">
  <strong>Note</strong>
  <p>This campaign is not focused on building new cycling infrastructure or promoting cycling as a transport mode. It is about recognising routes that people are already using and ensuring that the basic conditions for using them safely exist.</p>
</div>
