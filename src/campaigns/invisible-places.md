## What we're seeing

Some destinations in Kerry are consistently difficult to reach by public transport, even when they serve significant numbers of people with high-frequency travel needs. Hospitals, secondary schools, employment areas, and leisure facilities may be well-known landmarks while being effectively cut off from public transport access.

This campaign maps specific destinations and documents the quality of their transport connections — looking at frequency, timing, journey time, and ease of access.

## Key destination types

**Healthcare:** Hospital and clinic appointments often require travel at specific times that may not align with service timetables. Missed appointments due to transport barriers carry real health costs.

**Education:** Students travelling to secondary schools and further education colleges may face long journeys by public transport or depend entirely on school transport schemes.

**Employment:** If an employment area is not accessible by public transport, workers without cars are excluded from those jobs — a meaningful constraint in areas with high car dependency.

**Town centres:** Access to basic retail, banking, and services in market towns is a quality-of-life issue for people in surrounding rural areas.
