## What we're seeing

Many everyday journeys in County Kerry — to work, to hospital, to school, to a food shop — are straightforward by car and very difficult without one. These journeys are rarely counted or mapped in transport planning, making them effectively invisible to the agencies and officials who decide what services to run.

This campaign aims to document specific examples: the journey that takes 90 minutes by bus and 15 by car; the appointment that cannot be reached on public transport at all; the job that cannot be taken without a driving licence.

## Barriers

Common barriers identified so far:

- **Distance:** Many rural areas are more than 2km from any bus stop
- **Timing:** Services may not run at times that match work or appointment schedules
- **Connections:** Reaching many destinations requires changing between services that do not connect well
- **Frequency:** Infrequent services make return journeys impractical for many purposes

## Small improvements that could help

Not all invisible journeys require major infrastructure. Some could be improved by:

- Better connections between existing services at interchange points
- Demand-responsive services for areas with low but consistent need
- Improved timetable coordination between routes serving the same corridor
- Clear information about how to combine walking, cycling, and bus travel
